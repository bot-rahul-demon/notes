Engineering Mathematics
=======================
Coal India Limited (CIL) - Management Trainee | Systems Discipline (Paper-II, Professional Knowledge)
EXHAUSTIVE TOPIC & SUBTOPIC BREAKDOWN

Exam note: CIL MT is an objective (MCQ) computer-based test, conceptual-to-moderate
difficulty (easier than GATE). Emphasis on discrete math, linear algebra, calculus,
probability, and numerical methods at a direct, formula-application level. Items
tagged "(advanced - awareness)" are low priority. Cover every leaf point; none left.

==================================================================
PART A: DISCRETE MATHEMATICS
==================================================================

1. Mathematical Logic
   1.1 Propositional logic: connectives (AND, OR, NOT, implication, biconditional)
   1.2 Truth tables; tautology, contradiction, contingency
   1.3 Logical equivalences; De Morgan's laws
   1.4 Normal forms: CNF and DNF (concept)
   1.5 Rules of inference; validity of arguments
   1.6 First-order (predicate) logic: predicates, quantifiers (universal, existential)
   1.7 Nested quantifiers and negation of quantified statements

2. Set Theory and Relations
   2.1 Sets, subsets, power set, operations (union, intersection, difference, complement)
   2.2 Venn diagrams; inclusion-exclusion principle
   2.3 Cartesian product
   2.4 Relations: properties (reflexive, symmetric, antisymmetric, transitive)
   2.5 Equivalence relations and equivalence classes
   2.6 Partial order relations; Hasse diagrams
   2.7 Functions: injective, surjective, bijective; composition; inverse
   2.8 Countable and uncountable sets (concept)

3. Partial Orders and Lattices
   3.1 POSET; upper/lower bounds, LUB, GLB
   3.2 Lattices; bounded, distributive, complemented lattices
   3.3 Hasse diagram interpretation

4. Algebraic Structures
   4.1 Binary operations and properties (closure, associativity, identity, inverse)
   4.2 Semigroup and monoid
   4.3 Groups; abelian groups; subgroups
   4.4 Order of a group/element (concept); cyclic groups (awareness)

5. Graph Theory
   5.1 Graph terminology (vertices, edges, degree, path, cycle)
   5.2 Handshaking lemma
   5.3 Types: simple, complete, bipartite, regular, planar
   5.4 Connectivity; components
   5.5 Eulerian and Hamiltonian graphs (conditions)
   5.6 Trees and spanning trees; properties
   5.7 Graph coloring; chromatic number
   5.8 Matching (concept)
   5.9 Planar graphs; Euler's formula (V - E + F = 2)

6. Combinatorics
   6.1 Counting principles (sum and product rules)
   6.2 Permutations and combinations
   6.3 Pigeonhole principle
   6.4 Inclusion-exclusion (counting)
   6.5 Binomial theorem and coefficients
   6.6 Recurrence relations:
        6.6.1 Forming recurrences
        6.6.2 Solving linear homogeneous recurrences (characteristic equation)
        6.6.3 Particular solutions (concept)
   6.7 Generating functions (concept)

==================================================================
PART B: LINEAR ALGEBRA
==================================================================

7. Matrices and Determinants
   7.1 Types of matrices (diagonal, identity, symmetric, skew-symmetric, orthogonal)
   7.2 Matrix operations: addition, multiplication, transpose
   7.3 Determinant: properties and computation
   7.4 Inverse of a matrix; adjoint
   7.5 Rank of a matrix
   7.6 Elementary row operations

8. System of Linear Equations
   8.1 Homogeneous vs non-homogeneous systems
   8.2 Consistency and number of solutions (rank method)
   8.3 Gaussian elimination
   8.4 Cramer's rule

9. Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors
   9.1 Characteristic equation
   9.2 Eigenvalues and eigenvectors computation
   9.3 Properties (trace = sum, determinant = product of eigenvalues)
   9.4 Cayley-Hamilton theorem (statement/use)
   9.5 Diagonalization (concept)

10. LU Decomposition
    10.1 LU factorization concept and method
    10.2 Solving systems using LU

==================================================================
PART C: CALCULUS
==================================================================

11. Limits, Continuity, Differentiability
    11.1 Limits and standard limit results
    11.2 Continuity at a point and on an interval
    11.3 Differentiability; relation to continuity
    11.4 Derivative rules (product, quotient, chain)

12. Applications of Derivatives
    12.1 Maxima and minima (local and global)
    12.2 Critical points; first and second derivative tests
    12.3 Mean value theorems: Rolle's, Lagrange's

13. Integration
    13.1 Indefinite integrals; standard integrals
    13.2 Methods: substitution, by parts, partial fractions
    13.3 Definite integrals and properties
    13.4 Area under a curve (concept)

==================================================================
PART D: PROBABILITY AND STATISTICS
==================================================================

14. Probability
    14.1 Sample space, events; axioms of probability
    14.2 Conditional probability; multiplication rule
    14.3 Independent events
    14.4 Bayes' theorem and total probability
    14.5 Random variables: discrete and continuous
    14.6 Probability mass/density functions; CDF
    14.7 Expectation, variance, standard deviation

15. Probability Distributions
    15.1 Uniform distribution
    15.2 Binomial distribution
    15.3 Poisson distribution
    15.4 Normal distribution (properties, standard normal)
    15.5 Exponential distribution

16. Statistics
    16.1 Measures of central tendency: mean, median, mode
    16.2 Measures of dispersion: range, variance, standard deviation
    16.3 Correlation and regression (concept)

==================================================================
PART E: NUMERICAL METHODS
==================================================================

17. Numerical Solutions of Equations
    17.1 Non-linear equations:
         17.1.1 Bisection method
         17.1.2 Newton-Raphson method
         17.1.3 Secant / regula-falsi (concept)
    17.2 Linear systems:
         17.2.1 Gauss elimination
         17.2.2 Gauss-Seidel iterative method
    17.3 Convergence and error (concept)

18. Numerical Integration
    18.1 Trapezoidal rule
    18.2 Simpson's 1/3 rule
    18.3 Error in numerical integration (concept)

19. Numerical Solution of ODEs
    19.1 Single-step methods: Euler's method, Runge-Kutta (RK4)
    19.2 Multi-step methods (concept)
