Theory of Computation
=====================
Coal India Limited (CIL) - Management Trainee | Systems Discipline (Paper-II, Professional Knowledge)
EXHAUSTIVE TOPIC & SUBTOPIC BREAKDOWN

Exam note: CIL MT is an objective (MCQ) computer-based test, conceptual-to-moderate
difficulty (easier than GATE). Emphasis on definitions, automata construction,
language classification, closure properties, and standard results. Deep proofs are
NOT required; items tagged "(advanced - awareness)" are low priority. Cover every leaf point.

   1.4 Kleene star and plus; Sigma*
   1.5 Chomsky hierarchy overview (Type 0,1,2,3) and machine/grammar mapping

PART B: REGULAR LANGUAGES
==================================================================

2. Finite Automata
   2.1 DFA:
        2.1.1 Formal definition (5-tuple)
        2.1.2 Transition table and diagram
        2.1.3 String acceptance; language of a DFA
        2.1.4 DFA construction for given languages
        2.1.5 Dead/trap states
   2.2 NFA:
        2.2.1 Formal definition; multiple transitions
        2.2.2 Acceptance (existence of accepting path)
        2.2.3 NFA construction
   2.3 Epsilon-NFA: epsilon-closure; acceptance
   2.4 Conversions:
        2.4.1 NFA to DFA (subset construction)
        2.4.2 Epsilon-NFA to DFA
        2.4.3 State blow-up (concept)
   2.5 DFA minimization (equivalent/distinguishable states, table-filling)
   2.6 Moore and Mealy machines; inter-conversion (concept)

3. Regular Expressions
   3.1 Definition; operators and precedence
   3.2 Algebraic identities (basic)
   3.3 RE to finite automaton
   3.4 Finite automaton to RE (state elimination / Arden's theorem)

4. Properties of Regular Languages
   4.1 Closure properties (union, intersection, complement, concatenation, star, reversal)
   4.2 Pumping lemma for regular languages (statement and use to prove non-regularity)
   4.3 Myhill-Nerode theorem (concept)
   4.4 Decision properties: emptiness, finiteness, membership, equivalence
   4.5 Regular grammars (left-linear, right-linear) and equivalence to FA

==================================================================
PART C: CONTEXT-FREE LANGUAGES
==================================================================

5. Context-Free Grammars (CFG)
   5.1 Formal definition (variables, terminals, productions, start symbol)
   5.2 Derivations: leftmost and rightmost
   5.3 Parse trees and yield
   5.4 Ambiguity (ambiguous grammars; inherent ambiguity - concept)
   5.5 CFG design for given languages
   5.6 Simplification: removing useless symbols, epsilon-productions, unit productions
   5.7 Normal forms: Chomsky Normal Form (CNF); Greibach Normal Form (advanced - awareness)
   5.8 Left recursion and left factoring (link to parsing)

6. Pushdown Automata (PDA)
   6.1 Formal definition (7-tuple); stack operations
   6.2 Acceptance by final state vs empty stack (equivalence)
   6.3 NPDA construction from CFG
   6.4 Deterministic PDA and DCFL (concept)
   6.5 Equivalence of CFG and PDA
   6.6 NPDA more powerful than DPDA (statement)

7. Properties of Context-Free Languages
   7.1 Closure: closed under union, concatenation, star, reversal; NOT under intersection/complement
   7.2 CFL intersect Regular = CFL
   7.3 Pumping lemma for CFLs (statement; proving non-CFL)
   7.4 Decision properties: emptiness, finiteness, membership (CYK - concept)
